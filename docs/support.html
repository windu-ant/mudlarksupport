Frequently Asked Questions

How do I connect to a MUD?

Open MUDlark, tap the world selector, and either choose a server from the built-in list or tap "Add Custom World" to enter a hostname and port. Then tap Connect.

What are triggers, aliases, and timers?

Triggers watch incoming text for patterns and automatically perform actions (highlight text, play a sound, send a command, etc.). Aliases let you type a short command that expands into longer text — e.g., typing kk goblin could expand to kill goblin. Timers send commands or perform actions on a repeating schedule.

How do I enable text-to-speech?

On first use, MUDlark will show a prompt to enable text-to-speech. You can also enable or adjust it anytime in the app's Settings under the Accessibility section.

Can I connect to multiple MUDs at once?

Yes! MUDlark supports multiple simultaneous sessions. You can switch between them using the session tabs.

What is the proxy server?

iOS suspends apps when they go to the background, which can drop your MUD connection. The optional MUDlark proxy relay server keeps your connection alive and buffers any text you miss, delivering it when you return to the app.

Does MUDlark collect my data?

No. MUDlark has no analytics, no tracking, and no ads. All your data stays on your device. See our privacy policy for full details.

What iOS version do I need?

MUDlark requires iOS 17 or later. It works on both iPhone and iPad.

The colors don't look right / text is garbled

Make sure the MUD server you're connecting to supports ANSI color. Some servers require you to toggle color on with a command (e.g., ansi on or color on). If text looks garbled, try changing the encoding setting for that world.

I found a bug — how do I report it?

Email us at mudlarkmudclient@gmail.com with a description of the issue, the MUD server you were connected to, and any steps to reproduce. Screenshots are always helpful! You can also report bugs in our Discord.
